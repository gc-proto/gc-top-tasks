Direct deposit information

Veterans Affairs Canada (VAC) offers the convenience of direct deposit to clients who wish to have payments issued by VAC, deposited at a financial institution. VAC payments include:

  • Disability Benefits
  • War Veterans Allowance
  • Financial Benefits
  • Treatment Benefits
  • Veterans Independence Program Benefits
  • Vocational Rehabilitation

How can I request direct deposit or change my direct deposit information?

If you are registered for My VAC Account, you can add or update your direct deposit information now. If you're not registered, why wait? Register today. My VAC Account is a simple and secure way to do business online with Veterans Affairs Canada.

My VAC Account

OR

You can complete the Direct Deposit Request form.

Once completed, please send your form to:

Veterans Affairs Canada
PO Box 6000
Matane QC G4W 0E4

or drop it off to your local VAC office.

How do I change my address with Veterans Affairs Canada?

If you are registered for My VAC Account, you can change your address now. If you're not registered, why wait? Register today. My VAC Account is a simple and secure way to do business online with Veterans Affairs Canada.

My VAC Account

OR

You can update your residential and/or mailing address by telephone, mail or fax.

VAC Assistance Service

The VAC Assistance Service can provide you with psychological support. It is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. The service is for Veterans, former RCMP members, their families, and caregivers and is provided at no cost. You do not need to be a client of VAC to receive services.

Toll-free: 1-800-268-7708

TDD/TTY: 1-800-567-5803

Contact the Veterans Ombudsman

You have the right to be treated with respect, dignity, fairness and courtesy by Veterans Affairs Canada. If you feel that any of your rights have not been upheld or a decision is unfair, you have the right to make a complaint to the Veterans Ombud.
