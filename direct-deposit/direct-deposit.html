Direct deposit

Get payments from the Government of Canada deposited directly into your bank account.

You can:

  • Set up direct deposit
  • Change your payments to a different bank account
  • Report a late, incorrect or unexpected payment (best done by phone)

If you are receiving multiple payments, you may need to contact different government departments to update your information.

Services and information

Individuals in Canada

Tax refunds and credits, benefits, retirement pensions, Employment Insurance, student grants and loans

Individuals outside of Canada

Check available countries and payments, sign up or chage banking information

Businesses and other organizations

Direct deposit for payment of supplier invoices, business tax refunds, grants and contributions

Help with direct deposit

How to find your banking information

You can find your banking information at the bottom of a cheque. You can use a paper cheque or you can get an electronic void cheque using your online banking.

Make sure that the cheque is for the same bank account as where you want your direct deposit payments to go.

Reading your cheque

The digits at the bottom of your cheque are described below.

  • The first set of digits are a cheque number: not required
  • The second set of digits (5 digits) are the Branch number: required
  • The third set of digits (3 digits) are the Institution number: required
  • The last set of digits (various lengths) are the Account number: required

If you are not able to get a void cheque, contact or visit your financial institution. They can provide the bank account information to you. You must request a stamp from your financial institution if you are submitting a direct deposit enrolment form without attaching a void cheque.

Reporting a late, incorrect or unexpected payment

Contact the organization responsible for your payment if you:

  • Continue receiving cheques after signing up for direct deposit
  • Do not receive your payment when you expected tot
  • Receive a payment you were not expectingt
  • Believe your payment amount is incorrect

Signing up using a power of attorney

You can sign up for direct deposit for another person if you have power of attorney. Submit your power of attorney or trusteeship forms to the department or agency responsible for the person’s payments before trying to sign up for direct deposit on their behalf.

Getting payments in a joint bank account

You can get direct deposit payments in a bank account that is shared by two people. Make sure that the account is in the names of each person who is receiving payments from the government.

Signing up with multiple bank accounts

If you receive multiple payments from the government, you can usually ask that those payments be deposited in different bank accounts. However, you must use the same bank account for your tax refund and all credits and benefits that you receive from the Canada Revenue Agency.

Reporting a change of address

If you have moved, be sure to change your address with all of the government departments and agencies that provide services to you. Even if your bank account information hasn’t changed, it is important to keep your address up to date.
